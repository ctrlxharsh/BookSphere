## Detailed Findings

The Maulana Azad Library (MAL) at Aligarh Muslim University (AMU) is one of Asia's largest academic libraries, housing over 1.5 million items including a world-renowned collection of Persian, Arabic, Urdu, and Sanskrit manuscripts. The digital strategy for 2024-2026 focuses on a 'Heritage with High-Tech' approach. Key technical requirements include Bento Box search results, AI-powered chatbots, and WCAG 2.2 accessibility standards. Brand identity is rooted in 'AMU Green' and gold/bronze accents, utilizing the university seal's palm and crescent imagery. Architecturally, the library's seven-story modernist-Islamic structure provides a design blueprint for digital 'scrollytelling' and UI elements like arched frames. Digital resource integration includes Shodhganga and e-ShodhSindhu, while user-centric features prioritize personalized 'My Library' dashboards and mobile-first responsiveness to mitigate information overload.
